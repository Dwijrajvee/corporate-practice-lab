Level 1: The Battlefield

Before you argue, you must know where to argue. Understanding "Seat vs. Venue" separates the students from the lawyers.

1. Arbitration vs. Litigation

Litigation: Public Courts (Slow, Public).
Arbitration: Private Judge (Faster, Confidential, Paid). You choose this in the contract.

2. Seat vs. Venue

Seat: The Legal Home. (If Seat is London, English Courts control the case).
Venue: The Physical Location. (We can meet in Hotel Taj, Mumbai, but the Seat remains London).

3. Institutional vs. Ad-Hoc

Institutional: Managed by a center (SIAC, LCIA, MCIA). They handle admin.
Ad-Hoc: Lawyers manage it themselves. (Cheaper but messier).

4. Section 9 (Interim Relief)

Emergency protection from Court before the Arbitration starts (e.g., "Stop them from selling the asset!").
