Module 1: The Art of NDA Review

1. The "Public Domain" Exception

The most common mistake junior lawyers make is ignoring the exceptions. You cannot agree to keep secret something that is already on Google.

‚ö†Ô∏è The Trap: A client signs an NDA that says "ALL information provided is confidential."

‚úÖ The Fix: You must carve out information that:
  • Is already public.
  • Was already known to the receiver.
  • Is independently developed.

2. What if a Judge asks?

If a court orders your client to reveal information, they must be allowed to do so without being sued for breach of contract.

Bad Clause (Do not accept):

"Recipient shall NOT disclose Confidential Information to any third party for any reason whatsoever."

Good Clause (Market Standard):

"Recipient may disclose information if required by law or court order, provided they give prompt notice to the Disclosing Party."

3. The "Bet the Company" Clause

Indemnity is a promise to pay for losses. In GenCorp, we fight hard to limit this.

If you breach the NDA, should you pay for Lost Profits (which can be millions) or just Direct Damages (legal fees)?

Pro Tip: Always try to exclude "Indirect, Consequential, and Punitive Damages."
