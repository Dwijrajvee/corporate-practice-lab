Level 3: The Art of Negotiation

The Discom has sent a draft. It looks standard, but it's a trap. Your job is to fix it.

1. The Golden Rule: "Who controls the risk pays for it."

Before you redline, you must know what is fair. Here is the standard Market Practice for Risk Allocation:

Risk Event Who should bear it? Why?
Land Acquisition Delay Developer It's your job to build the plant.
Grid Unavailability Buyer (Discom) The Grid is their property. If it breaks, they pay.
Change in Law (Taxes) Buyer (Discom) Pass-through cost (reimbursed).
Solar Radiation Low Developer You chose the site/technology.

2. Redline Simulation: Spot the Trap

⚠️ Trap #1: Grid Availability

The Discom inserted this clause. Why is it dangerous?

"Clause 5.2: In the event of any Grid Failure or shutdown of the substation, the Developer shall NOT be entitled to any compensation or deemed generation payments."
⚠️ Trap #2: Termination Payment

They want to fire us. Look at the payout:

"Clause 13.1: If the Discom terminates this Agreement due to its own Default, it shall refund the Performance Bank Guarantee to the Developer."
