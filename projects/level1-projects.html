Level 1: The Jargon Buster

Before you draft a contract, you must speak the language. Here are the 4 terms that run the industry.

COD (Commercial Operation Date)

The project's "Birthday." Before COD, you are building. After COD, you are selling power. Missing this date means paying huge penalties.

Tariff (The Price)

The price of 1 Unit of electricity (e.g., ‚Çπ2.60/kWh). Once discovered in a bid, this is usually fixed for 25 years.

PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)

The marriage contract between the Generator (Solar Co) and the Buyer (Discom). Without a signed PPA, banks won't lend a single rupee.

Section 63 (The Shield)

A section of the Electricity Act. It says "If the price was found via transparent bidding, the Government MUST accept it."
